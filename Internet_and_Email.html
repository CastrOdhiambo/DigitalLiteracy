Section 6.1

What is the Internet?

Understanding the Internet

The internet is a global network of billions of computers and devices connected together. It allows information to travel instantly across the world. The World Wide Web (WWW) is the collection of websites and pages you access using the internet.

Cybercafés Still Exist

If you don't have home internet, visit a cybercafé. They charge per hour (usually 20–50 Ksh). Bring a USB drive to save your work.

1 Understanding Connection Types
  1. Ask yourself: What internet connection do I have at home? (Wi-Fi / Mobile Data / None)
  2. If you use mobile data, check your data balance by dialling *544# (Safaricom) or *123# (Airtel)
  3. Visit a nearby cybercafé and ask: "How much per hour?" and "Do you have printing?"

Knowing how to search effectively saves time and finds better results.

Trustworthy Sources

Not everything online is true

Always check: Who wrote the information? Is it a .gov (government) or .ac.ke (university) site? When was it published? Compare multiple sources before believing.

Reliable Kenyan Websites

Section 6.4

Email Accounts — Your Digital Identity

Why You Need an Email Address

  • Apply for jobs (CV submission)
  • Register for eCitizen (passports, driving license, business permits)
  • Open a bank account
  • Receive official communication
  • Sign up for online services (Jumia, KPLC, etc.)
  • Contact teachers, employers, or customers

Free Email Providers in Kenya

Gmail

Most popular. 15GB free storage. Works on any device. Sign up at mail.google.com

Outlook

Microsoft's email. Good for professional use. Outlook.com

Yahoo Mail

1TB storage. Older but still functional. Yahoo.com/mail

Creating a Gmail Account — Step by Step

Create Your Free Gmail
  1. 1 Go to mail.google.com or accounts.google.com/signup
  2. 2 Click Create account → For myself
  3. 3 Fill in: First name, Last name
  4. 4 Choose a username (e.g., john.mwangi2025) — this becomes your email: john.mwangi2025@gmail.com
  5. 5 Create a strong password (at least 8 characters: letters + numbers + symbols)
  6. 6 Confirm password
  7. 7 Click Next → enter phone number (for recovery) → Next → verify with code sent via SMS
  8. 8 Fill in recovery email (optional), birth date, gender → Next → Accept terms
Password Security Rules
  • NEVER share your password with anyone
  • Use different passwords for different accounts
  • Write your password in a safe place (not on a sticky note on your computer!)
  • Use a phrase: Mombasa2024@Kenya is stronger than "password123"
4 Create Your Email Account
  1. Follow the steps above to create a Gmail account
  2. Choose a professional username: firstname.lastname@gmail.com or firstname2025@gmail.com
  3. Create a strong password (write it down in a notebook — NOT on your computer)
  4. Add your phone number for password recovery
  5. Log in to your new Gmail account

Section 6.5

Sending and Receiving Emails

Composing an Email

Write and Send an Email (Gmail)
  1. 1 Log in to your Gmail
  2. 2 Click the Compose button (pencil icon)
  3. 3 In To field: type the recipient's email address
  4. 4 In Subject: write a brief summary of the email (e.g., "Application for Internship")
  5. 5 In the large box: write your message
  6. 6 Click Send button
To: hiring@company.co.ke
Subject: Application for Data Entry Position

Dear Hiring Manager,

I am writing to apply for the Data Entry position advertised on your website. I have completed Digital Literacy training and am proficient in Microsoft Word, Excel, and email communication.

I have attached my CV for your review.

Thank you for your consideration.

Sincerely,
Jane Akinyi

Reading and Replying to Emails

  • Inbox: All emails you receive appear here. Unread emails are bold.
  • Reply: Open an email → Click the Reply arrow (↩) → Type your response → Send
  • Reply All: Sends to everyone who received the original email (use carefully)
  • Forward: Share an email with someone else → Click Forward → type recipient → Send
  • Delete: Click the trash icon to move email to Trash

Organising Your Inbox

  • Labels (Gmail) / Folders (Outlook): Create folders to organise emails: "Job Applications", "Banking", "School", "Personal"
  • Archive: Remove from Inbox but keep for later (not deleted)
  • Star/Flag: Mark important emails for follow-up
  • Spam/Junk: Unwanted emails go here automatically. Check occasionally for legitimate emails mistakenly flagged.
5 Send Your First Email
  1. Log into your Gmail account
  2. Click Compose
  3. To: your-own-email-address (send to yourself for practice)
  4. Subject: "My First Email"
  5. Message: "Hello! I am learning how to use email. This is my first test message."
  6. Click Send
  7. Check your Inbox — you should see the email
  8. Open it and click Reply, type "This is my reply!" → Send

Section 6.6

Email Attachments — Sending Files

How to Attach a File

Attach a File to an Email
  1. 1 Click Compose to start a new email
  2. 2 Look for the paperclip icon 📎 (Attach files)
  3. 3 Click it → browse your computer for the file
  4. 4 Select the file → Click Open
  5. 5 Wait for the file to upload (a progress bar appears)
  6. 6 Write your message and send

Attachment Size Limits

  • Gmail: 25MB per email
  • Outlook: 20MB per email
  • If your file is too large, use Google Drive (for Gmail) or compress the file (ZIP)
Downloading Attachments

When you receive an email with an attachment, click the attachment name or download icon to save it to your computer. Be careful — only download from people you trust.

6 Send an Email with an Attachment
  1. First, create a simple Word document on your computer: type your name and save as My_CV.docx
  2. Compose a new email to yourself
  3. Subject: "CV for Job Application"
  4. Click the paperclip icon and attach My_CV.docx
  5. Send the email
  6. Check your Inbox, open the email, and download the attachment

Section 6.7 — Critical

Online Safety, Phishing & Scams

Common Online Threats in Kenya

Phishing Emails

Fake emails pretending to be from KRA, Safaricom, or your bank. They ask you to click a link and "verify" your password. Never click links in suspicious emails! Real organisations will never ask for your password by email.

Too-Good-To-Be-True Scams

"You won 500,000 Ksh!" "Free iPhone!" These are scams designed to steal your personal information. If it sounds too good to be true, it is a scam.

Identity Theft

Never share your ID number, PIN, or bank details online unless YOU initiated the contact on the official website. Scammers pretend to be government officials to steal your identity.

How to Spot a Phishing Email

Red FlagWhat to Look For
Generic greeting"Dear Customer" instead of your name
Urgent language"Your account will be closed in 24 hours!"
Suspicious sender addressfrom "kra@email-support.com" not "kra.go.ke"
Spelling/grammar errorsPoor English is common in scam emails
Requests for passwordNo real company asks for your password via email

Safe Browsing Habits

  • Look for HTTPS: Before entering passwords or bank details, check the address bar for 🔒 and "https://" (the 's' means secure)
  • Avoid public Wi-Fi for banking: Cybercafé Wi-Fi can be intercepted. Use mobile data for sensitive transactions.
  • Log out of accounts: Especially on shared computers (cybercafés). Click Sign Out.
  • Clear browsing history: On shared computers: Chrome → Three dots → History → Clear browsing data
  • Keep software updated: Browser updates include security fixes.

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

2FA adds an extra layer of security. After entering your password, you also enter a code sent to your phone. Enable this on your Gmail, bank account, and social media.

Enable 2FA on Gmail
  1. 1 Go to your Google Account → Security
  2. 2 Click 2-Step Verification
  3. 3 Follow steps to add your phone number
  4. 4 Now every login requires your password + a text message code
7 Security Practice
  1. Check if a website you use has HTTPS (look for 🔒 in address bar)
  2. Examine a recent email — does it look legitimate? Check the sender address carefully.
  3. On a shared computer: Clear browsing history (Chrome → Settings → Privacy → Clear browsing data)
  4. Enable 2-Factor Authentication on your Gmail account

Section 6.8 — Final Project

Complete Project: Apply for a Job by Email

Real-World Scenario: Job Application Email

A company has advertised a Data Entry position. You must send a professional email with your CV attached. This project tests everything you've learned.

Step 1 — Prepare Your CV
  • Open Microsoft Word and create a simple CV (use the CV template from Chapter 3)
  • Include: Your name, contact, education, skills, and a short profile
  • Save as YourName_CV.pdf (File → Save As → PDF)
Step 2 — Research the Company
  • Use Google to search for a real company in Kenya that might hire interns or data entry staff
  • Find their email address (usually on their "Contact Us" page or Careers page)
  • If you can't find a real one, use your own email address as practice
Step 3 — Write the Email
  • To: [company email address]
  • Subject: Application for Data Entry Position – [Your Name]
  • Message: Use the professional email format shown below
  • Attachment: Attach YourName_CV.pdf
To: careers@company.co.ke
Subject: Application for Data Entry Position – Jane Akinyi

Dear Hiring Manager,

I am writing to apply for the Data Entry position advertised on your website. I have completed Digital Literacy training and possess the following skills:

• Microsoft Word, Excel, and PowerPoint
• Fast and accurate typing (40 wpm)
• Professional email communication
• Ability to work under supervision

I have attached my CV for your review. I am available for an interview at your earliest convenience.

Thank you for considering my application.

Sincerely,
Jane Akinyi
Phone: 0712 345 678
Email: jane.akinyi@gmail.com

8 Complete Job Application Email
  1. Create your CV in Word and save as PDF
  2. Search for a real Kenyan company with a careers email (or use your own email for practice)
  3. Compose a professional email following the template above
  4. Attach your CV PDF
  5. Proofread: Check spelling, grammar, and the recipient's email address
  6. Send the email
  7. Check your Sent folder to confirm it was sent
  8. Create an email label/folder called "Job Applications" and move this email there
