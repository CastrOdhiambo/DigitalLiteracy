Section 7.1

eCitizen — Your Gateway to All Government Services

🏛️

eCitizen Kenya

The unified government portal for 100+ services — passports, IDs, business registration, driving licences, land, and more. One account accesses everything.

www.ecitizen.go.ke

Creating an eCitizen Account

Every Kenyan service on eCitizen requires an account. Help your customer create one before anything else.

Step-by-Step Account Registration
  1. 1 Open your browser → go to www.ecitizen.go.ke
  2. 2 Click "Register" at the top-right corner of the homepage
  3. 3 Enter National ID number, full name (exactly as on ID), email, and active phone number
  4. 4 Create a strong password (8+ characters, mix of letters, numbers, and symbols like @#$)
  5. 5 Check your email inbox → click the activation link sent by eCitizen (check Spam folder if not visible)
  6. 6 Enter the SMS code sent to the phone number to verify it
  7. 7 Login with email and password → you will see your eCitizen Dashboard
Cybercafé Tip

Write the customer's email and password on paper and give it to them. Many customers forget their credentials immediately. Also, always forward confirmation emails to the customer's phone via WhatsApp screenshots.

Passport Application — Step by Step

Direct link: https://immigration.ecitizen.go.ke

Documents Required

  • Original National ID card + scanned copy (both sides, PDF or JPG, max 2MB)
  • Original birth certificate + scanned copy
  • Passport photo — white background, clear face, no glasses, no shadows, digital copy max 500KB
  • Recommender's ID copy (someone who knows you — employer, teacher, or chief)
Full Passport Application Process
  1. 1 Login to eCitizen → select "Directorate of Immigration Services"
  2. 2 Click "Passport Application" → read all instructions carefully
  3. 3 Select passport type and number of pages
  4. 4 Fill the application form — personal details, place of birth, parents' details, profession, physical address
  5. 5 Upload passport photo (white background, max 500KB)
  6. 6 Upload scanned ID (both sides) and birth certificate
  7. 7 Review EVERY detail — wrong information causes rejection and you lose the fee
  8. 8 Select payment method → choose M-Pesa (Lipa na M-Pesa)
  9. 9 Note the PayBill number and account number shown on screen → customer pays from phone → enter M-Pesa transaction code on portal
  10. 10 After payment: download and print 3 copies of the application summary and 3 payment receipt copies
  11. 11 Book appointment at nearest immigration office (Nyayo House Nairobi / Mombasa / Kisumu)
  12. 12 Remind customer to bring: printed forms, original documents, and physical passport photos on the appointment date
Always Print 3 Copies of Everything

Immigration offices are strict — they require 3 copies of the application summary and 3 copies of the payment receipt. Charge the customer for printing. Do NOT let them leave without their printed documents.

Section 7.2

KRA iTax — Tax Compliance Portal

🧾

Kenya Revenue Authority — iTax

Register for a KRA PIN, file income tax returns, pay taxes, get tax compliance certificates, and check your tax status.

itax.kra.go.ke   PayBill: 572572

KRA PIN Registration — First Time

A KRA PIN is required for employment, opening a bank account, buying a car, registering a business, getting a passport, or doing any major transaction in Kenya. Every adult Kenyan needs one.

PIN Registration Steps
  1. 1 Visit https://itax.kra.go.ke
  2. 2 Click "New PIN Registration" under Quick Links
  3. 3 Select category: Individual (personal) or Non-Individual (company/partnership)
  4. 4 Enter National ID number, ID issue date, ID expiry date, and date of birth — must all match the ID exactly
  5. 5 Enter a valid, accessible email address and mobile number
  6. 6 Create and write down a strong password
  7. 7 Select tax obligations: Most individuals → "Income Tax – Resident Individual" only; Business owners earning under Ksh 5M/yr → add "Turnover Tax"; VAT registered → add "VAT"
  8. 8 Click Submit — KRA processes instantly
  9. 9 Check email → KRA sends PIN Certificate as an attachment within minutes
  10. 10 Download and print 2 copies for the customer
Customer Has No Email?

Register using a cybercafé email, then immediately forward the PIN certificate to the customer via WhatsApp or print it. Alternatively, help the customer create a Gmail account first (Chapter 6), then use that for KRA registration.

Filing Individual Income Tax Returns

Deadline: 30th June every year for the previous tax year. Late filing attracts a penalty of Ksh 2,000 per month. Even if you had zero income, you must file a NIL return — failure results in fines.

Employed Persons
  • Login to iTax with PIN and password
  • Returns → File Return → select tax year
  • Choose P1 form (employed resident individual)
  • System auto-fills from employer's monthly PAYE submissions
  • Verify the pre-filled information → Submit
  • Download and print the Acknowledgement Receipt
Self-Employed / Business
  • Login to iTax with PIN and password
  • Returns → File Return → select tax year
  • Choose P9 form (self-employed/business)
  • Enter gross income, allowable expenses, instalment taxes paid
  • System calculates tax due or refund
  • If tax due → generate payment slip → pay via M-Pesa PayBill 572572

Tax Compliance Certificate (TCC)

Required for government tenders, employment, and business contracts. Apply after filing all returns:

  • Login to iTax → Certificates → Apply for Tax Compliance Certificate
  • System checks all returns are filed and taxes paid
  • Certificate generated instantly if fully compliant
  • Valid for 12 months from date of issue
  • Download and print — no fee for the certificate itself
NIL Returns — File Even with Zero Income

Students, unemployed people, and housewives who have a KRA PIN must still file a NIL return every year by 30th June. Login to iTax → File Return → select year → choose NIL return → submit. It takes 2 minutes and avoids a Ksh 2,000/month penalty.

Section 7.3

KUCCPS — University & College Placement

📚

Kenya Universities and Colleges Central Placement Service

Apply for government-sponsored university and TVET college placement after KCSE results. Change course selections and track placement status.

students.kuccps.net   PayBill: 820201

Accessing the KUCCPS Student Portal

First-Time Login
  1. Visit students.kuccps.net
  2. Username: your KCSE Index Number
  3. Password: your KCSE Index Number (same as username initially)
  4. You will be prompted to change the password immediately
  5. Enter new password and confirm → save it in a safe place
Application Fee Payment

Total: Ksh 1,500 — paid before submitting course choices

  1. M-Pesa → Lipa na M-Pesa → PayBill
  2. Business No: 820201
  3. Account No: KCSE Year + Index Number (e.g., 2024 1234567890)
  4. Enter amount: 1500 → enter M-Pesa PIN → confirm
  5. Return to portal → confirm payment → select your courses

Applying for Courses — Step by Step

Course Application Process
  1. 1 Login to KUCCPS student portal with KCSE index number
  2. 2 Pay application fee (Ksh 1,500) via M-Pesa PayBill 820201 before selecting courses
  3. 3 Click "Apply for Programme"
  4. 4 Search for courses by name or browse by institution
  5. 5 Check your KCSE grade vs course cut-off points — only apply to courses you qualify for
  6. 6 Add up to 6 course choices in order of preference (first choice = most preferred)
  7. 7 Review your choices carefully → Submit application
  8. 8 Download and print the application acknowledgement
  9. 9 Check placement results when announced on the same portal
Revision Window — Change Your Choices

KUCCPS opens a revision window after initial application where students can change their course choices. Watch for announcements on the KUCCPS website and social media. This is often the most important visit to the cybercafé for students.

Section 7.4

HELB — Higher Education Loans Board

🎓

Higher Education Loans Board

Apply for student loans and bursaries, check loan balance, make loan repayments, and check loan clearance status for employment.

helb.co.ke   PayBill: 222222

First-Time Loan Application

HELB provides loans to students in Kenyan universities and TVET colleges. The loan covers tuition and upkeep.

Documents Required

  • National ID card (both sides, scanned)
  • KRA PIN Certificate
  • Valid, accessible email address
  • Active phone number
  • KCSE index number and year
  • KCPE index number
  • Recent passport photo (digital, white background)
  • Official university/college admission letter
  • Parents' National ID copies (both mother and father)
  • Two guarantors' National ID copies (not family members)
  • Birth certificate
HELB Loan Application Steps
  1. 1 Visit www.helb.co.ke → click "Student Portal"
  2. 2 First time: click "Register" → enter ID number, KCSE index, phone, email → create password
  3. 3 Login with your credentials → go to "Apply for Loan"
  4. 4 Enter personal details — must match your ID exactly
  5. 5 Enter parents' details and upload their ID scans
  6. 6 Enter guarantors' details and upload their ID scans
  7. 7 Upload admission letter (PDF, max 2MB)
  8. 8 Upload passport photo
  9. 9 Fill the financial background section honestly
  10. 10 Review and submit application
  11. 11 Download the loan application serial number — keep this safe
  12. 12 HELB processes in 4–6 weeks — track status via the student portal

HELB Loan Repayment (Graduates)

  • Graduates must begin repaying after getting employed
  • Repay via M-Pesa: PayBill 222222, Account: ID Number
  • Check balance and payment history in the student portal
  • Get HELB Clearance Certificate (needed for employment) from the portal once fully repaid

Vehicle Search — Before Buying a Car

This is one of the most important services at a cybercafé. Customers buying used vehicles must always verify ownership and status first.

Vehicle Search Steps
  1. 1 Login to eCitizen → select NTSA Services
  2. 2 Click "Motor Vehicle Search / Verification"
  3. 3 Enter the vehicle registration number (format: KXX 123A — no spaces)
  4. 4 Pay Ksh 550 via M-Pesa
  5. 5 Report shows: Make/model, Year, Engine & chassis numbers, Current owner name, Any loans/encumbrances on the vehicle, Colour, and accident history
  6. 6 Download and print for the customer — they should compare with the logbook

Google Drive — Your Free Cloud Office

☁️

Google Drive

15GB of free cloud storage. Store, create, share, and access your documents, photos, and files from any computer, phone, or tablet — anywhere in Kenya, anytime.

drive.google.com

What Is Google Drive and Why Every Kenyan Needs It

Google Drive is Google's free cloud storage service. Think of it as a hard drive in the sky — your files live on Google's servers, not just on one computer. This means even if your laptop is stolen, broken, or lost at the cybercafé, your documents are safe and accessible from any device with internet access.

Every Gmail account comes with 15GB of free storage — enough for thousands of Word documents, PDFs, and photos. For most Kenyans, 15GB is more than enough for years of documents.

Why Drive Is Essential
  • Never lose your CV, certificates, or KRA PIN — they're safe in the cloud
  • Access your files from any cybercafé computer without a flash disk
  • Share documents with employers by sending a link instead of an attachment
  • Collaborate — multiple people can edit one document at the same time
  • Automatic saving — Drive saves every change as you type, no more Ctrl+S panic
What's Included in Drive
  • 📄 Google Docs — like Microsoft Word, free
  • 📊 Google Sheets — like Microsoft Excel, free
  • 📊 Google Slides — like Microsoft PowerPoint, free
  • 📝 Google Forms — create surveys and questionnaires
  • 📁 File storage — upload any file type

Accessing Google Drive

Open Google Drive
  1. 1 Open Chrome → type drive.google.com in the address bar → press Enter
  2. 2 If not already signed in → enter your Gmail address and password
  3. 3 Your Drive homepage opens — showing "My Drive" with all your files and folders
  4. 4 OR — click the Google Apps grid (⋮⋮⋮) at the top-right of any Google page → click Drive

Uploading Files to Google Drive

Upload any file from your computer to Drive and it becomes accessible from any device forever — as long as you have your Gmail login.

Method 1 — Drag and Drop
  1. Open drive.google.com
  2. Open File Explorer in another window
  3. Find your file (e.g., your CV)
  4. Drag it directly into the Google Drive browser window
  5. It uploads automatically — watch the progress bar

Fastest method for uploading one or a few files.

Method 2 — New Button
  1. Click the + New button (top-left of Drive)
  2. Select File upload
  3. Browse to your file → select it → click Open
  4. The file uploads and appears in My Drive

To upload an entire folder: click Folder upload instead.

Creating Folders and Organising Your Files

Just like on your computer, Google Drive lets you create folders to keep your files organised. A well-organised Drive means you can always find what you need in seconds.

Create a Folder in Drive
  1. 1 Click + New → select New Folder
  2. 2 Type a folder name (e.g., My CV & Certificates) → click Create
  3. 3 The folder appears in My Drive — double-click to open it
  4. 4 Drag files into the folder, or upload directly into it using + New → File Upload

Sharing Files from Google Drive

Sharing via Drive is better than email attachments for large files. You send a link — the recipient clicks it and sees or downloads the file instantly. No file size limits, no bounce-backs.

Share a File or Folder
  1. 1 Right-click the file or folder in Drive → select Share
  2. 2 A sharing dialog opens — type the recipient's email address, OR click "Copy link"
  3. 3 Set the permission level:
    Viewer — can only view, not edit
    Commenter — can add comments
    Editor — can make changes to the file
  4. 4 Click Send (by email) or Copy link (to share via WhatsApp or any other way)
Share Your CV with Employers the Professional Way

Upload your CV to Google Drive → right-click → Share → Change to "Anyone with the link can view" → Copy link → paste the link in your job application email. The employer clicks the link and sees a crisp, perfectly formatted document. No attachment size issues, no corrupt files.

Be Careful with "Anyone with the link" Setting

If you set a file to "Anyone with the link can view," anyone who gets that link — even someone you didn't intend — can see it. For sensitive documents like your ID scan or bank statements, use "Restricted" sharing (specific people only) or just download and attach the file directly.

Google Forms — Collect Information Online

📝

Google Forms

Create professional surveys, registration forms, quizzes, feedback forms, and job application forms in minutes — completely free. Responses are collected automatically in Google Sheets.

forms.google.com

What Is Google Forms and Who Uses It?

Google Forms is a free online tool that lets you create digital forms — questionnaires, surveys, registration forms, quizzes, and feedback forms. Instead of printing paper forms and collecting them manually, you share a link. Anyone who clicks the link fills in the form online, and all responses appear instantly in your Google account.

Who Uses Google Forms in Kenya?
  • Schools — Student registration, exam feedback, parent surveys
  • Churches & NGOs — Event registration, volunteer sign-ups
  • Businesses — Customer feedback, order forms, job applications
  • Government — Community data collection, needs assessment
  • Students — Research questionnaires, group project planning
  • Cybercafés — Customer registration, service feedback
Why Forms Is Powerful
  • 100% free — no subscription needed
  • No paper, no printing, no manual data entry
  • Responses collected automatically in Google Sheets
  • Works on any phone or computer — no app to download
  • Real-time response graphs and charts built-in
  • Share via link, WhatsApp, email, or QR code

Creating Your First Google Form — Step by Step

Create a Google Form
  1. 1 Go to forms.google.com (or Google Drive → + New → Google Forms)
  2. 2 Click "Blank form" to start from scratch, OR choose a template (Event registration, Contact form, RSVP, Quiz, etc.)
  3. 3 Click "Untitled form" at the top → type your form title (e.g., Youth Club Registration — Kisumu 2026)
  4. 4 Add a description below the title to explain the form's purpose
  5. 5 Click on the first question → type it (e.g., What is your full name?)
  6. 6 Choose the question type from the dropdown on the right
  7. 7 Toggle "Required" on questions that must be answered
  8. 8 Click the + button on the right panel to add more questions
  9. 9 Click the eye icon (Preview) to see how your form looks to respondents
  10. 10 Click the purple Send button → choose how to share: by email, copy link, or embed code

Canva Design Tips — Look Professional Every Time

Good Design Habits
  • Stick to 2 fonts maximum per design — one for headings, one for body text
  • Use 2–3 colours that work together — Canva's colour palettes help
  • Leave white space — don't crowd everything together
  • Use high-quality photos — Canva has millions of free stock images built in
  • Make the most important information the largest text
  • Always include contact information on business materials
Design Mistakes to Avoid
  • Too many fonts — makes design look chaotic and unprofessional
  • Too many colours — pick a palette and stick with it
  • Tiny text — if printed, must be readable from arm's length
  • Stretching images — always resize from corners to maintain proportions
  • Forgetting to proofread — spelling errors on printed materials are embarrassing
  • Using a PNG for a design that needs printing — always use PDF Print

Online Safety, Fraud Prevention & Best Practices

Protecting Yourself and Your Customers Online

Always Do These
  • Use incognito / private browsing mode for all government portal work
  • Log out completely after every session — click "Logout", not just closing the tab
  • Clear browser history after every customer (Ctrl+Shift+Delete)
  • Verify the URL starts with https:// — the padlock icon must be visible
  • Write down credentials on paper and give to customer before they leave
  • Ask for the customer's National ID before handling any government transaction
  • Keep a log of transactions — date, service, customer ID, amount charged
  • Charge fairly and give receipts for all services
Never Do These
  • Never save customer passwords in the browser — always decline "Save Password"
  • Never leave a customer's government portal session open unattended
  • Never click links from emails claiming to be from KRA, eCitizen, or HELB — go directly to the website
  • Never share a customer's personal information with third parties
  • Never help customers with clearly illegal transactions
  • Never install unknown software to "help" access a portal — these are viruses
  • Never scan unknown USB drives without antivirus scanning first
  • Never store customer ID numbers, PINs, or passwords in a spreadsheet on the cybercafé computer

Common Online Scams Targeting Kenyans

Educate every customer about these scams — they are widespread and cost Kenyans millions of shillings every year.

Scam TypeHow It WorksHow to Avoid It
Fake eCitizen WebsitesFraudulent websites that look exactly like eCitizen collect login credentials and personal informationAlways type the URL manually: www.ecitizen.go.ke. Check the padlock. Never click links from SMS or WhatsApp.
KRA Tax Refund ScamSMS/email saying "You have a tax refund of Ksh 25,000 — click here to claim"KRA never sends refund notifications by SMS or WhatsApp. Ignore and delete. Report to 020-4999-999.
Fake Job Offers"You have been selected for a government job — pay Ksh 2,000 registration fee to proceed"Government jobs are FREE to apply. PSC and TSC never charge fees. Any fee request is a scam.
M-Pesa SIM SwapFraudsters call pretending to be Safaricom, asking you to "confirm your M-Pesa PIN" to process a SIM swap, then steal moneyNever share your M-Pesa PIN by phone, SMS, or in person — not even to someone claiming to be from Safaricom.
Fake LogbooksVehicles sold with forged logbooks — buyer pays for a car that belongs to someone else or has outstanding loansAlways do an NTSA vehicle search (Ksh 550) on eCitizen before paying any deposit on a vehicle.
HELB Loan Scam"We can get you a HELB loan faster for Ksh 3,000 — give us your portal login details"HELB applications are FREE and direct. Never give your portal credentials to anyone.
