Section 7.1

e-Citizen: Your Gateway to Government Services

Creating an e-Citizen Account

e-Citizen (www.ecitizen.go.ke) is the unified payment platform for accessing Kenyan government services. Most customers will need it for passport applications, driving licenses, business registration, and land transactions.

StepActionDetails
1Visit the portalOpen your browser and go to www.ecitizen.go.ke
2Click "Register"Located at the top right corner of the homepage
3Enter personal detailsNational ID number, full names as they appear on ID, email address, mobile phone number
4Create passwordMust be strong: at least 8 characters with letters, numbers, and symbols
5Verify emailCustomer opens email from e-Citizen and clicks activation link
6Verify mobileEnter the code sent via SMS to confirm the phone number
7LoginUse registered email and password to access dashboard

Passport Application (Immigration Services)

Direct link: https://immigration.ecitizen.go.ke

Passport Application Steps
  1. 1 Login to e-Citizen using customer's credentials
  2. 2 Select "Immigration Services" under "Directorate of Immigration Services"
  3. 3 Choose "Passport Application" – Read instructions carefully before proceeding
  4. 4 Select passport type: Ordinary 32 pages (Ksh 4,550), 48 pages (Ksh 6,050), 64 pages (Ksh 7,550), East African (Ksh 990)
  5. 5 Fill application form with personal details, place of birth, parents' details, ID number, profession
  6. 6 Upload photo: white background, clear face, no glasses, max 500KB
  7. 7 Upload documents: National ID (both sides), Birth certificate, Recommender's ID copy
  8. 8 Review information – Double-check EVERY detail – errors cause rejection
  9. 9 Make payment via M-Pesa PayBill, Visa/Mastercard, or bank
  10. 10 Download forms: Application summary (3 copies), Payment receipts (3 copies)
  11. 11 Book appointment at Nyayo House, Mombasa, or Kisumu
Cybercafe Tip: Always print three copies of everything. Charge for scanning documents if customer brings hard copies.

Section 7.2

KRA iTax: Tax Compliance Portal

KRA PIN Registration

KRA iTax (https://itax.kra.go.ke) handles all tax matters. PIN is needed for bank accounts, employment, business registration, vehicle transfer, and land transactions.

PIN Registration Steps
  1. 1 Visit iTax portal → https://itax.kra.go.ke
  2. 2 Click "New PIN Registration" under "Quick Links"
  3. 3 Select category: Individual – for personal PIN; Non-Individual – for companies
  4. 4 Enter National ID number, ID issue date, ID expiry date, Date of birth
  5. 5 Enter contact details: Valid email address, mobile phone number
  6. 6 Create password – Strong password – note it down for customer
  7. 7 Select tax obligations: For most individuals: "Income Tax – Resident Individual" only
  8. 8 Submit application – Check email for PIN certificate within minutes
  9. 9 Download PIN certificate and print at least 2 copies

Filing Individual Income Tax Returns

Deadline: Every year by 30th June for the previous year's income.

StepActionDetails
1Login to iTaxUse customer's PIN and password
2Go to "Returns" menuClick "File Return" under "Returns" dropdown
3Select return periodChoose tax year (e.g., 2024 for returns due June 2025)
4Select return formP1 – Resident Individual (employed); P9 – Business/self-employed
5For employed personsSystem auto-fills from employer's monthly filings – just verify
6For self-employedEnter gross sales, expenses, instalment taxes paid
7Tax computationSystem calculates tax due or refund owed
8SubmitDownload "Return Acknowledgement Receipt" as proof

Section 7.3

KUCCPS & HELB: Education Portals

KUCCPS: University and College Placement

Portal link: https://students.kuccps.net

KUCCPS Application Process
  1. 1 Visit students.kuccps.net
  2. 2 First time: Use KCSE index number as username and password
  3. 3 Reset password immediately after first login
  4. 4 Browse available courses and institutions
  5. 5 Select and rank course choices
  6. 6 Pay application fee: Ksh 1,500 via M-Pesa PayBill 820201
  7. 7 Submit application and print confirmation

HELB: Higher Education Loans Board

HELB (https://www.helb.co.ke) provides loans and bursaries to Kenyan students.

First-Time Loan Application Requirements
  • National ID (or waiting card)
  • KRA PIN certificate
  • Valid email address and phone number
  • KCSE index number and year
  • KCPE index number and year
  • Passport size photo (digital)
  • Admission letter from university/college
  • Parents' National ID copies
  • Two guarantors' ID copies
  • Birth certificate

Section 7.4

Productivity & Creative Tools

Canva: Professional Design Made Simple

Website: https://www.canva.com – Create CVs, flyers, posters, social media graphics.

Creating a Professional CV/Resume
  1. 1 Go to canva.com and create free account
  2. 2 Search "CV" or "Resume" in templates
  3. 3 Browse templates and select one
  4. 4 Replace text with customer details
  5. 5 Upload photo if needed
  6. 6 Customize colors and fonts to match style
  7. 7 Download as PDF (best for printing)

CapCut & PDF Tools

CapCut (https://www.capcut.com) – Free video editor for social media content, event videos.

ILovePDF (https://www.ilovepdf.com) – Merge PDF, Compress PDF, PDF to Word, Word to PDF.

Compressing Images and PDFs
  1. 1 For images: Visit tinypng.com → Upload image → Download compressed version
  2. 2 For PDFs: Visit ilovepdf.com/compress_pdf → Upload PDF → Choose compression level → Download

Section 7.6

Cybercafe Best Practices & Security

Professional Customer Service

  • Ask for ID – Before handling sensitive transactions, verify customer identity
  • Explain steps – Keep customer informed about what you are doing
  • Offer printed receipts – Always provide printed confirmation of applications
  • Keep a log – Maintain a record of transactions for reference
  • Charge fairly – Be transparent about service fees

Security Practices

  • Clear browser history – After each customer, clear all browsing data (Ctrl+Shift+Delete)
  • Use incognito/private mode – For sensitive transactions, use Chrome's incognito mode
  • Don't install unknown software – Only use trusted applications
  • Scan USBs – Always scan customer USBs before opening files
  • Log out completely – Ensure all portals are logged out before next customer
