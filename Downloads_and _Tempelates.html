Service Application Checklists

Kenyan Passport Application

Required for adult first-time applicants via eCitizen. Ensure all documents are original + 2 certified copies.

  • ✅ National ID — original + 2 photocopies
  • ✅ Birth Certificate — original + 2 photocopies
  • ✅ Recommender's National ID photocopy (must be passport holder)
  • ✅ 6 passport photos — white background, recent, 35×45mm
  • ✅ eCitizen payment receipt — print 3 copies (Ksh 4,550 standard)
  • ✅ Application summary form — print 3 copies from eCitizen
  • ✅ Appointment slot confirmation — print or screenshot
  • ✅ Previous passport (if renewal)

HELB First-Time Student Loan

For students applying for a HELB undergraduate loan for the first time.

  • ✅ National ID (original) — or KCSE index number if under 18
  • ✅ KRA PIN certificate — printout
  • ✅ University or college admission letter
  • ✅ Birth Certificate — original
  • ✅ KCSE certificate or result slip
  • ✅ KCPE certificate or result slip
  • ✅ Parent/Guardian National ID copies (both parents if possible)
  • ✅ Two guarantors — National ID copies + their HELB PIN details
  • ✅ Passport photo (digital, for upload)
  • ✅ Save your HELB serial number (sent by SMS) — never lose it

KRA PIN Registration

Required for employment, banking, and government services. Apply free at itax.kra.go.ke.

  • ✅ National ID — number and copy
  • ✅ Valid, accessible email address
  • ✅ Active mobile phone number
  • ✅ Date of birth (matches ID)
  • ✅ Physical address (postal or residential)
  • For business registration:
  • ✅ Certificate of incorporation or business name registration
  • ✅ KRA PINs of all directors
  • ✅ CR12 document

National ID (Turning 18)

Apply at your nearest Huduma Centre or Chief's office within 90 days of your 18th birthday.

  • ✅ Birth Certificate — original
  • ✅ School leaving certificate (KCSE or KCPE)
  • ✅ Chief's letter confirming identity and village
  • ✅ Parent's/guardian's ID copies (both if possible)
  • ✅ 2 passport photos (white background)
  • ✅ KCSE/KCPE registration details (index number)

NTSA Driving Licence (New)

Apply through the eCitizen NTSA portal. Must have completed an approved driving school course.

  • ✅ National ID — original + photocopy
  • ✅ KRA PIN certificate
  • ✅ Driving school certificate of completion
  • ✅ Passport photos — 2 copies (white background)
  • ✅ Medical form D1 (from registered clinic)
  • ✅ eCitizen account (apply via ecitizen.go.ke → NTSA)
  • ✅ Payment receipt (Ksh 3,050 via eCitizen/M-Pesa)

Job Application Email Checklist

Run through this checklist before clicking Send on any job application email.

  • ✅ Recipient email address is correct (double-check!)
  • ✅ Subject line: "Application for [Position] – [Your Full Name]"
  • ✅ Greeting: "Dear Mr./Ms. [Name]" or "Dear Hiring Manager"
  • ✅ Paragraph 1: Who you are and which position you're applying for
  • ✅ Paragraph 2: Your relevant skills and experience
  • ✅ Paragraph 3: Request for interview + availability
  • ✅ Closing: "Sincerely, [Your Full Name]"
  • ✅ Signature includes phone number and email
  • ✅ CV attached as PDF (not Word) — named YourName_CV.pdf
  • ✅ Proofread: no spelling or grammar errors

Cybersecurity & Online Safety Guide

🔐 The Golden Rules of Online Safety

Every internet user in Kenya must know these rules. Print this section and pin it near every computer in your home, cybercafé, or classroom. These rules protect your money, your identity, and your digital life.

Password Safety Rules

  • ✅ Use at least 12 characters — longer is stronger
  • ✅ Mix: UPPERCASE, lowercase, numbers (1–9), symbols (!@#$%)
  • ✅ Use a different password for each account
  • ✅ Enable 2-Factor Authentication (2FA) on Gmail and eCitizen
  • ✅ Write your password in a notebook — store it at home, privately
  • ❌ Never use: your name, birth year, "password", "123456"
  • ❌ Never share your password with anyone — ever
  • ❌ Never type your password on a public screen others can see
  • ❌ Never save passwords in a browser on a shared computer

Strong example: Ng0ma!Safar1&254

Recognise Phishing & Scams

Phishing is when criminals pretend to be KRA, eCitizen, your bank, or Safaricom to steal your details.

  • 🚨 Fake sender: kra-refund.net — real KRA uses kra.go.ke
  • 🚨 "URGENT — your account will be closed in 24 hours"
  • 🚨 "You have won Ksh 1,000,000 — pay processing fee first"
  • 🚨 "Click here to verify your KRA PIN immediately"
  • 🚨 "Safaricom M-Pesa reversal" — share your PIN to "confirm"
  • 🚨 "Hi cousin, new number — send me Ksh 2,000 urgently"
  • 🚨 "Send M-Pesa first, I'll deliver the phone"
Rule: If it sounds too good to be true — it IS a scam.

Cybercafé & Public Computer Safety Rules

Always Log Out
Before leaving any public computer, log out of Gmail, eCitizen, and any other account. Do not just close the browser.
Check HTTPS
Before entering a password or ID, check for the padlock 🔒 and https:// in the address bar. Never enter sensitive details on HTTP sites.
Bring a USB Drive
Save your work on a USB drive. Do not leave files on the café's computer — they may be seen or deleted by the next user.
Clear Browser History
After using a public computer, press Ctrl+Shift+Delete → Clear browsing data, passwords, and cookies.
Antivirus Is Active
Ensure Windows Defender is on. Never disable antivirus to install software from unknown websites.
Private Browsing
On shared computers, use Incognito mode: Ctrl+Shift+N. History and passwords are not saved when you close the window.
Quick Fixes

Troubleshooting Desk Reference

For Cybercafé Operators & Trainers

Print this section and laminate it. Place one copy at the operator's desk and one near each workstation. It answers 90% of questions students ask during computer sessions.

"No Internet" — What to Do

  1. Check the Wi-Fi icon in the taskbar (bottom right) — is it connected?
  2. Click the Wi-Fi icon → Check if the correct network is selected
  3. Forget the network and reconnect: enter the password again
  4. Restart the router — unplug from power for 30 seconds, plug back in
  5. Check if other devices have internet (phone, another computer)
  6. If mobile data: dial *544# (Safaricom) to check your data balance
  7. Still nothing? Restart the computer entirely
  8. Call your ISP if the issue persists more than 30 minutes

"File Won't Upload on Portal"

  1. File too large? eCitizen max = 2MB, KRA iTax max = 5MB. Compress it (see Compression Guide below)
  2. Wrong format? Most portals require JPG (photos) or PDF (documents)
  3. Filename issue? Remove spaces and special characters: rename to MyID_Photo.jpg
  4. Browser issue? Try Chrome instead of Edge or Firefox
  5. Cache problem? Press Ctrl+Shift+Delete → clear cache → try again
  6. Session timeout? Log out completely, log back in, and try fresh
  7. Internet speed? Large files need a stable connection — avoid mobile data for uploads

"Forgot My Password" — Account Recovery

  • Gmail: Go to accounts.google.com → click "Forgot password" → use recovery phone number or backup email
  • eCitizen: Login page → "Forgot Password" → enter registered email → check inbox for reset link
  • KRA iTax: "Forgot Password" → enter your KRA PIN and email → check email for reset
  • HELB: Use your National ID number and registered phone number to reset
  • NTSA: "Forgot Password" → registered email or phone
Prevention: Write your email + password in a notebook. Keep it locked at home.

"Document Won't Print"

  1. Check that the printer is powered on and connected (cable or Wi-Fi)
  2. Check the printer screen or light — is it showing an error?
  3. Is there paper in the tray? Is it loaded correctly (not jammed)?
  4. Check ink levels — does the printer have a "low ink" light?
  5. On the computer: Start → Devices and Printers → right-click your printer → "See what's printing" → cancel any stuck jobs
  6. Try restarting the print spooler: Start → type "services" → find Print Spooler → right-click → Restart
  7. Restart the printer and computer as a last resort

"Computer Running Very Slowly"

  1. Close browser tabs you're not using — each tab uses memory
  2. Close applications running in the background (taskbar, bottom right)
  3. Press Ctrl+Shift+Esc → Task Manager → End tasks using high CPU
  4. Restart the computer — clears memory and fixes many issues
  5. Check disk space: File Explorer → This PC — if C: drive is nearly full, delete old files or empty Recycle Bin
  6. Run Windows Defender scan: Start → "Windows Security" → Quick Scan
  7. Disable startup programs: Task Manager → Startup tab → disable unnecessary apps

"Can't Find Email / Email Not Arriving"

  1. Check Spam / Junk folder — government and portal emails often go there
  2. Check All Mail label (Gmail) — emails may be archived rather than deleted
  3. Use Gmail search bar — type the sender's name or subject keywords
  4. Check you gave the correct email address when registering
  5. Wait — some portal emails (KRA, eCitizen) can take up to 15 minutes
  6. Check your internet connection — no connection = emails don't load
  7. If the email you sent was undelivered, check your Sent folder — did it actually send?

"Excel Formula Showing Error"

ErrorMeaning & Fix
#DIV/0!Dividing by zero or empty cell. Check the divisor cell isn't blank.
#VALUE!Wrong data type — mixing text and numbers. Check all cells are numbers.
#REF!Reference is broken (cell was deleted). Re-enter the formula.
#NAME?Misspelled formula name. Check spelling: =SUM not =SOM
########Column too narrow to show the number. Widen the column.
#N/AVLOOKUP can't find the value. Check the lookup value exists.

"USB / Flash Drive Not Detected"

  1. Remove the USB, wait 5 seconds, plug it back in firmly
  2. Try a different USB port on the computer
  3. Open File Explorer (Win+E) — does the drive appear?
  4. Right-click the Start button → Disk Management — is the drive listed? Right-click → Assign Drive Letter
  5. Try the USB on another computer — if it doesn't work there either, the drive may be damaged
  6. Run USB on your own computer first to check for viruses before using in cybercafé

File Compression Guide — Reduce File Sizes

Why Compress Files?

eCitizen, KRA iTax, HELB, and NTSA portals all have upload size limits. If your file is too large, the portal will reject it. Here is exactly how to make any file smaller:

Compress Photos (TinyPNG)

Best for: passport photos, ID scans, any JPG/PNG image for portal upload.

  1. Go to tinypng.com in Chrome
  2. Drag and drop your image onto the page (or click to upload)
  3. TinyPNG compresses it automatically — usually 60–80% smaller
  4. Click Download to save the smaller version

Result: 2MB photo → reduced to under 500KB in seconds. Free, no login needed.

Compress PDF (iLovePDF)

Best for: scanned documents, certificates, multi-page PDFs too large for portals.

  1. Go to ilovepdf.com/compress_pdf
  2. Click Select PDF files and upload your document
  3. Choose compression level: "Recommended compression"
  4. Click Compress PDF — wait a moment
  5. Click Download compressed PDF

Result: 10MB scan → reduced to under 2MB. Free, no signup required.

Resize Photo Using MS Paint

Works offline — no internet needed. Best for photos that are too large in dimensions.

  1. Right-click the image → Open with → Paint
  2. Click Resize (in the Home ribbon)
  3. Select Percentage → change to 50% (or less)
  4. Click OK → File → Save As → choose JPEG

Halving dimensions reduces file size to roughly 25% of the original.

Create a ZIP File (Multiple Files)

Combine and compress multiple files into one ZIP for sending by email or uploading.

  1. Select all the files you want to compress (hold Ctrl and click each)
  2. Right-click on any selected file
  3. Choose "Send to" → "Compressed (zipped) folder"
  4. A .zip file is created in the same location
  5. Rename it clearly (e.g., JaneAkinyi_Documents.zip)
