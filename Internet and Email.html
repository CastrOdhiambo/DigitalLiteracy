Internet and Email - Terminologies and Basic Concepts

PART V: INTERNET & EMAIL (PRACTICAL)

Introduction
The Internet is one of the most powerful tools in the digital world. It connects millions of computers worldwide and allows people to communicate, learn, work, and do business online.

In this chapter, you will learn:
What the Internet is, Important Internet terminologies, How to connect to the Internet, How to use a web browser.

This chapter is highly practical, and you are encouraged to practice every step.

6.1 Understanding the Internet and Basic Terminologies

A. What is the Internet?

The Internet is a global network of interconnected computers that communicate and share information.

In simple terms, it allows people around the world to:
Search for information, Send and receive emails, Watch videos, Study online, Use social media, Conduct business.

B. Important Internet Terminologies

  • 1. Website – A collection of related web pages under one name. Example: www.google.com
  • 2. Web Page – A single page within a website.
  • 3. Web Browser – Software used to access websites. Examples: Google Chrome, Microsoft Edge, Mozilla Firefox, Safari.
  • 4. URL (Uniform Resource Locator) – The address of a website. Example: https://www.university.edu
  • 5. Search Engine – A website that helps you find information online. Examples: Google, Bing, Yahoo.
  • 6. Download – Transferring a file from the Internet to your computer.
  • 7. Upload – Sending a file from your computer to the Internet.

6.2 Connecting to the Internet (Practical)

A. Connecting Using Wi-Fi

Steps:

  1. Click the Network/Wi-Fi icon on the taskbar.
  2. Select a Wi-Fi network from the list.
  3. Click Connect.
  4. Enter the Wi-Fi password.
  5. Click Next/Connect.
  6. Wait until it shows Connected.

B. Connecting Using Mobile Hotspot

Steps:

  1. Turn on Mobile Data on your phone.
  2. Go to Settings → Hotspot & Tethering.
  3. Turn on Mobile Hotspot.
  4. On your computer, click the Wi-Fi icon.
  5. Select your phone's hotspot name.
  6. Enter the hotspot password.
  7. Click Connect.

Practical Activity 1

  1. Connect your computer to a Wi-Fi network.
  2. Open a web browser.
  3. Type www.google.com in the address bar.
  4. Press Enter.
  5. Confirm that the page loads successfully.
Tips for Beginners: Always connect to secure (password-protected) networks. Avoid entering personal information on unknown public Wi-Fi. Check your connection status before browsing.
Common Problems and Solutions:
No Internet access → Restart router
Cannot connect → Re-enter correct password
Slow Internet → Move closer to router
Website not loading → Refresh page

6.3 Using a Web Browser Effectively (Very Practical)

Introduction
A web browser is your main tool for accessing the Internet. Knowing how to use it properly will make your online experience faster, safer, and more productive.

A. Opening a Web Browser

Common browsers include: Google Chrome, Microsoft Edge, Mozilla Firefox, Safari.

Steps to Open a Browser:
I. Click the Start Menu. II. Type the name of the browser (e.g., Chrome). III. Click the browser icon to open it.
OR
I. Double-click the browser icon on the desktop.

B. Understanding the Browser Interface

When the browser opens, you will see:
Address Bar → Where you type website addresses (URLs)
Search Bar → Used to search for information
Tabs → Allow multiple websites to be open at once
Back and Forward Buttons → Navigate between pages
Refresh Button → Reloads the page

C. Searching for Information

You can search in two main ways:
Method 1: Using the Address Bar
I. Click inside the address bar. II. Type your search term (e.g., Importance of digital literacy). III. Press Enter. IV. Review the search results.
Method 2: Using a Search Engine Website
I. Type www.google.com in the address bar. II. Press Enter. III. Type your search term in the search box. IV. Click Search or press Enter.

D. Opening and Managing Tabs

To Open a New Tab: I. Click the + (New Tab) button. OR II. Press Ctrl + T on the keyboard.
To Switch Between Tabs: I. Click the desired tab. OR II. Press Ctrl + Tab.
To Close a Tab: I. Click the X on the tab. OR II. Press Ctrl + W.

E. Bookmarking a Website

Steps: I. Open the website you want to save. II. Click the Star icon in the address bar. III. Choose a folder (e.g., Bookmarks Bar). IV. Click Done.
To open a bookmarked site: I. Click the Bookmarks Menu. II. Select the saved website.

F. Downloading Files

Steps: I. Click the file link (e.g., PDF or image). II. Click Download. III. Choose location (e.g., Downloads folder). IV. Click Save. V. Open the file from the Downloads folder.

Practical Activity 1

  1. Open your browser.
  2. Search for "Benefits of Computer Literacy."
  3. Open two different websites in separate tabs.
  4. Bookmark one website.
  5. Download a PDF file from one of the sites.
  6. Locate the downloaded file in your Downloads folder.
Internet Safety Tip: Do not download files from unknown or suspicious websites. Always check the website address carefully. Avoid clicking on pop-up advertisements.
Common Mistakes to Avoid: Typing search terms in the wrong place, Opening too many tabs and getting confused, Downloading unsafe files, Forgetting to bookmark important websites.

6.4 Creating and Using Email (Very Practical)

Introduction
Email (Electronic Mail) is a method of sending and receiving messages over the Internet.

It is used for:
Formal communication, Job applications, School assignments, Business communication, Sending documents and files.

A. Creating an Email Account (Using Gmail Example)

There are many email providers such as: Gmail (www.gmail.com), Outlook (www.outlook.com), Yahoo Mail. We will use Gmail as an example.

Steps to Create a Gmail Account:

  1. Open your web browser.
  2. Type www.gmail.com in the address bar.
  3. Click Create account.
  4. Choose For myself (or appropriate option).
  5. Enter your details: First name, Last name, Username (this becomes your email address), Password.
  6. Click Next.
  7. Enter your phone number (optional but recommended).
  8. Verify with the code sent to your phone.
  9. Agree to the Terms and Conditions.
  10. Your Gmail account will open successfully.

Example Email Address: john123@gmail.com

B. Signing In to Your Email

It is the process of gaining access to your email account.

Steps: I. Go to www.gmail.com. II. Enter your email address. III. Click Next. IV. Enter your password. V. Click Sign in.

C. Understanding the Email Interface

When you open Gmail, you will see:
Inbox → Received messages, Compose → Create a new email, Sent → Messages you have sent, Drafts → Unfinished messages, Trash → Deleted emails.

D. Sending an Email

Steps: I. Click Compose. II. In the To field, type the recipient's email address. III. In the Subject field, type the topic of the email. IV. Type your message in the message box. V. Click Send.

Example of a Simple Email

Subject: Assignment Submission

Dear Sir/Madam,

Please find attached my assignment for review.

Thank you.
Best regards,
Your Name

E. Attaching a File to an Email

Steps: I. Click Compose. II. Click the Attach (Paperclip) icon. III. Select the file from your computer. IV. Click Open. V. Wait for the file to upload. VI. Click Send.

F. Replying and Forwarding Emails

To Reply: I. Open the email. II. Click Reply. III. Type your message. IV. Click Send.
To Forward: I. Open the email. II. Click Forward. III. Enter the new recipient's email address. IV. Click Send.

Practical Activity 1

  1. Create a Gmail account (if you do not have one).
  2. Log in to your email.
  3. Compose a new email and send it to a friend.
  4. Attach a Word document or PDF file.
  5. Ask your friend to reply.
  6. Check your Inbox and reply back.

Email Etiquette (Very Important)

  • Always include a clear subject line
  • Use polite and professional language
  • Avoid typing in ALL CAPITAL LETTERS
  • Do not share your password
  • Always log out on public computers
Common Mistakes to Avoid: Sending emails without a subject, Attaching the wrong file, Forgetting to attach a file mentioned in the message, Sharing login details with others.

6.5 Internet Safety, Security, and Digital Responsibility (Very Important)

Introduction
The Internet is powerful and useful, but it also has risks. Every digital user must understand how to stay safe, protect personal information, and behave responsibly online.

This section is highly practical and essential for all Internet users.

You will learn: How to protect personal information, How to create strong passwords, How to identify online threats, How to behave responsibly online.

A. Protecting Personal Information

Personal information includes: Full name, Phone number, Home address, School or workplace, Bank details, Passwords.

Practical Safety Rules:

  • Do not share passwords with anyone.
  • Avoid posting personal details publicly on social media.
  • Do not enter personal information on suspicious websites.
  • Always check website addresses carefully.
  • Log out from accounts when using public computers.

B. Creating Strong Passwords

A strong password protects your account from hackers.

Characteristics of a Strong Password:
At least 8-12 characters, Combination of uppercase letters, Lowercase letters, Numbers, Special symbols (@,#,$,%).

Example of strong password format: Digi@2026Learn

Steps to Change Password: I. Log in to your account. II. Go to Account Settings. III. Click Security.

C. Identifying Online Threats

1. Phishing – Fake messages pretending to be from banks, companies, or friends asking for personal information.
Warning Signs: Urgent message like "Your account will be closed!", Suspicious links, Poor spelling or grammar.

2. Malware – Malicious software that can damage your computer.
Prevention: Do not download from unknown websites, Install antivirus software, Avoid clicking suspicious ads.

3. Scams and Fraud – Fraudsters may promise money, prizes, or job offers.
Rule: If it sounds too good to be true, it probably is.

D. Safe Browsing Practices

Steps to Stay Safe Online:

  • Use trusted websites only.
  • Look for "https" in the website address.
  • Keep your browser updated.
  • Install antivirus software.
  • Avoid clicking unknown links in emails.

E. Digital Responsibility and Online Behavior

Being responsible online means: Respecting others, Avoiding cyberbullying, Not spreading false information, Following laws and regulations.

Good Digital Citizenship Includes:

  • Thinking before posting online.
  • Respecting other people's opinions.
  • Not sharing harmful or inappropriate content.
  • Reporting suspicious or harmful activities.

Practical Activity 1

  1. Review your social media privacy settings.
  2. Change your password to a stronger one.
  3. Check if your browser shows "https" on websites you visit.
  4. Delete any suspicious emails in your inbox.
  5. Install or update antivirus software.
Common Mistakes to Avoid: Using easy passwords like 123456, Clicking unknown email links, Sharing personal information publicly, Ignoring software updates.
