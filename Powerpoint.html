Microsoft PowerPoint is presentation software that allows you to create slide shows with text, images, charts, and animations. It is used in schools for student presentations, in businesses for proposals and reports, and in churches for worship lyrics. A well-designed PowerPoint presentation communicates your message clearly and professionally.

In this chapter, you will learn everything from creating your first slide to delivering a complete presentation with transitions, animations, and professional design.

Section 5.2

Creating and Managing Slides

Slide Layouts

PowerPoint provides pre-designed slide layouts for different content types. Choose the right layout for each slide.

LayoutBest Used For
Title SlideFirst slide — presentation title and subtitle
Title and ContentMost common — heading plus bullet points, images, or table
Section HeaderSeparate different parts of your presentation
Two ContentCompare two items side by side (text + image, or two images)
BlankComplete freedom — design from scratch

Adding New Slides

Shortcut

Press Ctrl+M to insert a new slide immediately

Ribbon

Home Tab → New Slide button → Choose layout

Duplicate Slide

Right-click a slide thumbnail → Duplicate Slide

Reordering, Deleting, and Hiding Slides

  • Reorder: Drag slide thumbnail up or down in the Slides Pane
  • Delete: Right-click slide thumbnail → Delete Slide
  • Hide: Right-click → Hide Slide (slide won't show during presentation but remains in file)
  • Reset: If you mess up formatting, right-click slide → Reset Slide
2 Create a Multi-Slide Presentation
  1. Open new blank presentation
  2. Slide 1: Title Slide → Title: "My Hobbies", Subtitle: "By [Your Name]"
  3. Press Ctrl+M → Slide 2: Title and Content → Title: "My Favourite Sport"
  4. Press Ctrl+M → Slide 3: Two Content → Title: "My Favourite Foods"
  5. Reorder: Move Slide 3 to become Slide 2
  6. Save as My_Hobbies

Section 5.3

Adding and Formatting Text

Working with Text Boxes and Placeholders

Each slide layout has placeholders — click inside to start typing. You can also add independent text boxes anywhere on the slide.

Add a Text Box
  1. 1 Insert Tab → Text Box
  2. 2 Click and drag on the slide to draw the box
  3. 3 Start typing

Formatting Text — Same as Word

Text formatting in PowerPoint works exactly like Microsoft Word:

Ctrl+B
Bold
Ctrl+I
Italic
Ctrl+U
Underline
Ctrl+L/E/R
Left/Centre/Right Align

Bullet Points — Essential for Presentations

Audiences remember bullet points, not paragraphs. Keep text concise — use short phrases, not complete sentences.

Golden Rule of PowerPoint

Never put full paragraphs on slides. Use 5-7 bullet points maximum per slide. The audience should listen to you, not read your slides.

Section 5.4

Inserting Images, Shapes, and SmartArt

Inserting Pictures

Add an Image
  1. 1 Insert Tab → Pictures → This Device
  2. 2 Browse to find your image
  3. 3 Click Insert
  4. 4 Resize using corner handles, drag to reposition
Picture Tools

When you select an image, the Picture Format tab appears — use it to crop, add borders, adjust brightness, or apply artistic effects.

Inserting Shapes and Icons

Shapes add visual interest to presentations — arrows, rectangles, circles, banners, and callouts.

Add a Shape
  1. 1 Insert Tab → Shapes
  2. 2 Choose a shape (e.g., Rounded Rectangle)
  3. 3 Click and drag on the slide
  4. 4 Use Shape Format tab to change fill colour, outline, and effects
  5. 5 Right-click → Add Text to type inside the shape

SmartArt — Professional Diagrams in One Click

SmartArt creates professional diagrams: process flows, hierarchies, cycles, matrices, and lists. Perfect for showing steps, organisation charts, or relationships.

Insert SmartArt
  1. 1 Insert Tab → SmartArt
  2. 2 Choose a category (Process, Cycle, Hierarchy)
  3. 3 Select a layout → OK
  4. 4 Type your text in the text pane

Section 5.5

Slide Transitions — Movement Between Slides

What Are Transitions?

Transitions are animations that play when you move from one slide to the next during a presentation. They add professional polish — but use them sparingly.

Fade Push Wipe Morph Zoom
Apply a Transition
  1. 1 Select the slide you want to apply the transition to
  2. 2 Go to Transitions Tab
  3. 3 Choose a transition from the gallery
  4. 4 Click Preview to see it
  5. 5 To apply to ALL slides, click Apply to All
Transition Tips
  • Use the SAME transition throughout — consistency looks professional
  • Avoid distracting transitions (e.g., random, vortex, glitter)
  • Fade and Push are the most professional choices
  • Set Duration to 0.5–1.0 seconds — not too fast, not too slow

Transition Timing Settings

  • Duration: How long the transition takes (shorter = faster)
  • Advance Slide: On Mouse Click (manual) OR After (automatic timing)
  • Apply to All: Use the same transition and timing for every slide
5 Add Transitions
  1. Open My_Hobbies
  2. Apply the Fade transition to Slide 2
  3. Click Apply to All so all slides use Fade
  4. Set Duration to 0.75 seconds
  5. Click Preview to see the effect
  6. Save

Section 5.6

Animations — Movement Within a Slide

What Are Animations?

Animations control how elements appear, move, or disappear on a single slide. Use animations to reveal bullet points one by one — keeping the audience focused on you, not reading ahead.

Appear Fade In Fly In Zoom Wipe
Apply an Animation
  1. 1 Select the text box or image you want to animate
  2. 2 Go to Animations Tab
  3. 3 Choose an animation from the gallery
  4. 4 Use Effect Options to customise direction (e.g., Fly In From Left)
  5. 5 Click Preview to see it

Animation Types

TypeDescriptionBest Used For
EntranceElement appears on screenMost common — revealing bullet points
EmphasisElement changes colour/sizeHighlighting a key point
ExitElement disappearsRemoving items from screen
Motion PathsElement moves along a pathShowing movement or direction
Animation Order — Start Settings
  • On Click: Animation plays when you click the mouse (best for bullet points)
  • With Previous: Plays at the same time as previous animation
  • After Previous: Plays automatically after previous animation finishes

Animation Pane — Manage Multiple Animations

Click Animation Pane on the Animations tab to see a list of all animations on the current slide. You can reorder, delete, or change timing from here.

6 Add Animations to Bullet Points
  1. Open My_Hobbies
  2. On Slide 2, select all bullet points (the entire text box)
  3. Apply Fade In animation
  4. Open Animation Pane — notice each bullet has its own animation
  5. Change Start to On Click for each bullet
  6. Click Preview — each bullet appears when you click
  7. Save

Section 5.7

Presenting and Printing Slides

Starting a Slide Show

From Beginning

Press F5 to start from Slide 1

From Current Slide

Press Shift+F5 or click the Slide Show icon on status bar

Presentation Controls During Show

Key / ActionWhat It Does
→ or ↓ or NNext slide / next animation
← or ↑ or PPrevious slide
BBlack screen (B for Black) — press again to resume
WWhite screen — press again to resume
EscEnd slide show
Right-clickOpen shortcut menu with Go to Slide, Pointer Options

Using the Pen / Highlighter During Presentation

Annotate on Slides
  1. 1 Right-click during slide show
  2. 2 Pointer Options → Pen or Highlighter
  3. 3 Draw on the screen
  4. 4 Press E to erase all annotations
  5. 5 Press Esc to stop drawing

Printing PowerPoint Slides

Print Options
  1. 1 Press Ctrl+P or File → Print
  2. 2 Under Settings, choose what to print:
Print LayoutWhat It PrintsBest Used For
Full Page SlidesOne slide per pageHandouts for review
Notes PagesSlide + speaker notes belowYour own reference during presentation
OutlineOnly text from slidesQuick content review
Handouts (3 slides per page)3 slides with lines for notesAudience handouts — most common
Handouts (6 or 9 slides)Compact layoutSaving paper
Save as PDF for Sharing

File → Save As → choose PDF (*.pdf) → Save. This preserves formatting for email or WhatsApp sharing.

7 Run Your Slide Show
  1. Press F5 to start your My_Hobbies presentation from the beginning
  2. Click through slides — watch the animations work
  3. Right-click → Pointer Options → Highlighter, highlight one word
  4. Press E to erase highlights
  5. Press Esc to end the show
  6. File → Print → Handouts (3 slides per page) → Print Preview
  7. Save as PDF: File → Save As → PDF

Section 5.8 — Real-World Project

Complete Presentation: "Digital Literacy in Kenya"

Final Project — Build a Complete 6-Slide Presentation

This project combines everything you have learned: slide creation, text formatting, images, transitions, animations, and professional design.

Slide 1 — Title Slide
  • Title: "Digital Literacy in Kenya"
  • Subtitle: "Empowering Citizens Through Technology"
  • Your name and date
  • Add a relevant background image or shape
Slide 2 — What is Digital Literacy?
  • Title: "What is Digital Literacy?"
  • Definition: "The ability to use computers, the internet, and software effectively."
  • Bullet points: "Accessing information", "Communicating online", "Using productivity tools", "Staying safe online"
  • Add an icon or image representing technology
Slide 3 — Benefits of Digital Skills
  • Title: "Why Digital Skills Matter"
  • Use a SmartArt Process diagram showing: Education → Employment → Business → Community Development
  • Or use 4 bullet points with icons
Slide 4 — Common Digital Tools
  • Title: "Essential Software"
  • Two Content layout: Left side list Microsoft Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Internet. Right side add an image of computer/software icons
Slide 5 — Challenges in Kenya
  • Title: "Overcoming Digital Barriers"
  • Bullet points: "Affordable devices", "Internet access", "Digital training centres", "Power supply reliability"
  • Add a callout shape with a solution for each challenge
Slide 6 — Conclusion / Thank You
  • Title: "Thank You"
  • Quote: "Digital literacy is the key to Kenya's future."
  • Contact information placeholder
  • Add a simple shape or image

Apply Professional Design

Design Theme
  1. 1 Design Tab → choose a professional theme (e.g., "Ion", "Gallery", "Facet")
  2. 2 Apply the same transition (Fade) to all slides
  3. 3 Add Fade In animation to all bullet points (On Click)
  4. 4 Ensure consistent fonts (Calibri or Arial throughout)
8 Build Your Complete Presentation
  1. Open new blank presentation
  2. Create all 6 slides following the structure above
  3. Apply a consistent design theme
  4. Add at least 3 images (from your computer or online search within PowerPoint)
  5. Apply the same transition to all slides
  6. Add entrance animations to bullet points (On Click)
  7. Check spelling: Review Tab → Spelling
  8. Press F5 and practise delivering the presentation
  9. Save as Digital_Literacy_Kenya
  10. Also save as PDF for sharing
